Zenni Optical

Problem

No platform. No roadmap. No precedent.

Zenni had no B2B2C platform, no enterprise portal, and no way for organizations to offer eyewear benefits to their employees. Healthcare companies, universities, and eye care providers needed a scalable solution — but the infrastructure to serve them didn't exist.

Gathered Insights

Getting close to the customer — on site and on stage.

  • Traveled to enterprise client sites — healthcare companies, universities, and eye care providers — to understand their workflows, pain points, and benefit administration processes firsthand
  • Worked directly with account managers to join client conversations and translate business requirements into product decisions
  • Represented the platform at Shoptalk Las Vegas and Vision Expo New York — presented to 500+ industry leaders, gathered real-time market feedback, and used booth conversations to shape the roadmap
  • Identified three distinct enterprise needs that could be served on a shared platform: Vision Benefits, Safety Eyewear, and Corporate Gifting

Solution

Three product lines on one enterprise platform.

  • Vision Benefits — employer-sponsored program, direct shipping, no cost to organization
  • Safety Eyewear — ANSI Z87.1-certified, OSHA compliant, self-serve portal eliminating paper vouchers
  • Corporate Gifting — custom branded eyewear and accessories, 3-step process from consultation to delivery
  • AI-powered computer vision product using facial scan data for personalized eyewear recommendations
  • Enterprise analytics layer giving clients real-time utilization data — became deciding factor in contract sign-offs
  • Self-serve onboarding portal cutting client time-to-value from weeks to minutes

Zenni Business portal — live demo

Implementation Process

Leading cross-functional execution from zero.

  • Led Engineering, Design, QA, Analytics, and AI/ML teams across the full product lifecycle — no playbook, built the process as we went
  • Established cross-functional cadences, dependency tracking, and decision frameworks from scratch
  • Compressed launch timelines by 2 months through disciplined dependency management and stakeholder alignment
  • Automated onboarding workflows saving 500 hours annually and reducing admin effort by 30%

Handling Challenges

Selling while building. No safety net.

  • Every product decision — pricing, onboarding flow, compliance framework — was a first. Had to define strategy, validate with clients, and execute simultaneously
  • Presented a product that was still being built to enterprise buyers at Shoptalk and Vision Expo — used those conversations to directly shape the roadmap in real time
  • Navigated AI/ML complexity — partnered closely with the ML team to define the computer vision product specification and ensure it delivered genuine value in enterprise demos

Customer Advocacy

In the room with the customer — always.

  • Visited enterprise client sites across healthcare, university, and eye care sectors to understand operational realities before making product decisions
  • Partnered with account managers on client calls — not just to gather requirements but to build relationships that accelerated deals
  • Used Shoptalk and Vision Expo booth conversations as continuous discovery — every interaction informed a product decision
