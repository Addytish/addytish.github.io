Problem

No Mini Cart. High abandonment. Revenue left on the table.

Rodan + Fields had no embedded checkout experience. Cart abandonment was high, cross-sell opportunities were being missed, and mobile checkout friction was directly impacting revenue across a 500K+ user base.

Gathered Insights

User research before a single line of code.

  • Conducted remote user research sessions to map the full checkout journey and identify highest-friction moments
  • Ran funnel analysis to pinpoint exact drop-off stages and quantify the revenue impact
  • Analyzed competitive mobile cart experiences across beauty and DTC brands to identify best practices
  • Validated assumptions about what a Mini Cart needed to include to actually change behavior — not just check a feature box

Solution

Mini Cart from zero — with personalization layered on top.

  • Led Mini Cart build from zero — embedded checkout integrating full payment flow, promotional savings, perks display, and subscription options in one seamless experience
  • Launched personalized promotions for 500K+ users based on purchase history and subscription tier
  • Owned subscription tier structures and auto-ship billing logic to support retention mechanics
  • A/B tested every checkout change — validated against cart abandonment and cross-sell metrics before shipping

Rodan + Fields Mini Cart — live product

Implementation Process

Research → prototype → test → ship.

  • Started with user research to validate the problem before defining the solution
  • Worked with design to prototype the Mini Cart UX — focused on reducing steps and surfacing promotions at the right moment
  • Ran A/B tests at each stage — nothing shipped without data backing
  • Coordinated Engineering, Design, and Analytics across the full build and launch cycle

Prototyping & UI Design

Designing for mobile-first behavior.

  • Collaborated with design on Mini Cart UX — focused on one-thumb reachability, clear promo visibility, and subscription tier clarity
  • Iterated on promotional display format based on user research findings — what users saw vs what drove action
  • Ensured the perks and savings display was prominent enough to reduce abandonment without cluttering the checkout flow
